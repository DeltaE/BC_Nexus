## Transport based vs Object oriented Model Structure

1. **Transport-based models**:
   - **Focus**: These models emphasize the movement or transport of energy carriers (such as electricity, heat, or fuels) throughout the system.
   - **Approach**: They typically employ a bottom-up approach, where energy flows and conversion processes are represented in detail within the system.
   - **Usage**: Transport-based models are widely used for analyzing energy systems, assessing various scenarios, and informing policy decisions. They are particularly suitable for understanding the dynamics of energy supply and demand.

 > [OSeMOSYS](https://osemosys.readthedocs.io/en/latest/) / [CLEWs](http://www.osimosys.org/) framework based model that are written in GNUMathprog are examples of such model. Our BC-Nexus is built on OSeMOSYS/CLEWs framework.

2. **Object-oriented models**:
   - **Focus**: Object-oriented models focus on representing entities within a system as objects with properties and behaviors.
   - **Approach**: They utilize principles of object-oriented programming to create a modular and flexible representation of the system, often allowing for easier scalability and extension.
   - **Usage**: Object-oriented models are employed in various domains, including software engineering, simulation, and system analysis. In the context of energy systems, they can be used to model complex interactions between different components or entities, such as power plants, consumers, and infrastructure.

 > [PyPSA](https://pypsa.readthedocs.io/en/latest/) is an example of such model.

 > [TransitionZero](https://www.transitionzero.org/) is developing a objected-oriented approach for OSeMOSYS framework and named it [tz-osemosys](https://github.com/transition-zero/tz-osemosys).
